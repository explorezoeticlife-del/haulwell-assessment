Driver Readiness Card
Life After CDL — Staying Steady When the Road Gets Real
New Driver
Remember this: Passing CDL training prepares you to operate the truck. Life on the road requires learning how to operate yourself under pressure.
Watch for these signals
Urine darker than pale yellow — you're already behind on hydration
Shorter fuse than usual — irritability is a fatigue signal
Can't sleep even when you have time — stress is running high
Feeling "flat" but not quite tired — that's margin shrinking
Saying yes to runs you're not sure about — fatigue talks you into things
Caffeine isn't working the same way it used to
Do these first
Drink water before reaching for caffeine — always
Make one realistic food swap per day — not a diet, just one thing
Name your margin level each morning — full, half, low, or empty
Protect at least one thing that recharges you each week
Find one veteran driver and ask them one real question
Track your blood pressure at least once a month — get a cuff
Blood pressure
You won't feel it early — that's the risk. These things push it up on the road:
Dehydration  ·  Salt-heavy food  ·  Poor sleep  ·  Chronic stress
None of that is your fault — it's built into the job. Knowing is the first step.
Hydration reality
The simple check: pale yellow = good. Darker = you're already behind.
Dehydration causes: headaches, irritability, foggy thinking, and blood pressure creep — before thirst even kicks in.
Target: a water bottle per 2–3 hours of driving.
Fatigue & caffeine
Fatigue accumulates like a slow leak. You don't feel it building — you just run out of margin.
Caffeine borrows energy from tomorrow. A big energy drink at 2am will ask for that back — with interest.
Natural reset: water, a short walk, 15 minutes of rest.
Your margin meter
Check in with yourself daily. Where is your margin right now — the space between "functioning" and "running on empty"? Name it before your body names it for you.
